## SLIDE 2 — 01/05

**mistake #1: your phone boots before you do**
the problem: You open your eyes and grab the phone. Before your feet hit the floor, you've fed your brain 40 other people's priorities.
why it matters: Dopamine spikes first thing set the baseline for the day. Everything slower than a feed now feels like work.
the fix: No inputs before output. Charge the phone across the room and log every phone-free morning for 7 days.

## SLIDE 3 — 02/05

**mistake #2: your wake time is random noise**
the problem: 6:30 on Tuesday, 9:00 on Sunday. Your first data point of the day changes every single morning.
why it matters: Wake time anchors your circadian clock. If the anchor drifts, energy, hunger and focus drift with it.
the fix: Pick one wake time and hold it within 30 minutes, weekends included. Track hits for 7 days first.

## SLIDE 4 — 03/05

**mistake #3: you stacked a 12-step miracle morning**
the problem: Ice bath, journaling, yoga, reading, meditation, all before 7am. By Wednesday the whole stack collapses.
why it matters: Every step is a failure point. When one breaks, you write off the entire morning and the data goes to zero.
the fix: Run one keystone habit for 90 days. Nail it daily, then add the next variable. One input at a time.

## SLIDE 5 — 04/05

**mistake #4: caffeine the second you're vertical**
the problem: Coffee hits your hand before sunlight hits your eyes. You're masking sleep pressure instead of clearing it.
why it matters: Adenosine is still draining when you wake. Caffeine on top of it buys you a crash by 2pm.
the fix: Delay the first cup 60-90 minutes and get outside light first. Log your 2pm energy for a week.

## SLIDE 6 — 05/05

**mistake #5: you never log the first hour**
the problem: You track workouts and macros, but the morning runs untracked. What did you do before 9am yesterday?
why it matters: The first hour predicts the next ten. If it's not logged, you can't debug the days that fall apart.
the fix: Track one binary: did the morning protocol happen, yes or no. 30 days of data beats 30 days of vibes.
