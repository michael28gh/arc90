## SLIDE 2 — 01/05

**mistake #1: your phone is inside the blast radius**
the problem: Your phone sits within arm's reach during every focus block. Each glance is a dopamine hit you never logged.
why it matters: Proximity predicts use better than intention does. You're running a focus protocol inside a distraction lab.
the fix: Move the phone to another room during deep work. Distance is friction, and friction is free willpower. Track phone-free blocks like any other metric.

## SLIDE 3 — 02/05

**mistake #2: betting on willpower in a rigged room**
the problem: You rely on discipline while junk food, open tabs and noise sit in plain sight. Willpower loses to environment on a long enough timeline.
why it matters: Every visible temptation is a background process eating your focus. Decision fatigue drains you by noon.
the fix: Remove the top 3 temptations from sight tonight. Design the default so the good choice is the lazy choice. Re-test for 7 days.

## SLIDE 4 — 03/05

**mistake #3: zero cues for the habit you want**
the problem: Your running shoes live in a closet. Your book lives in a drawer. The habit has no trigger in physical space.
why it matters: Out of sight is out of protocol. Cues start behavior loops — no cue, no rep, no data.
the fix: Stage the habit the night before: shoes by the door, book on the pillow, water on the desk. Make the first step visible and stupid-easy.

## SLIDE 5 — 04/05

**mistake #4: one space, five conflicting protocols**
the problem: You work, scroll, eat and sleep in the same two meters. Your brain can't tell which mode it's supposed to run.
why it matters: Context is a cue. When every zone triggers everything, no zone triggers anything.
the fix: Assign one function per zone — even a single chair counts. Bed is for sleep only. Watch your sleep latency improve within a week.

## SLIDE 6 — 05/05

**mistake #5: you never log the environment variable**
the problem: You track the habit but not the conditions around it. A missed day looks like weakness when it was really a changed room.
why it matters: You can't debug what you don't measure. Environment is the hidden confound wrecking your streak data.
the fix: When you miss, log where you were and what changed. Patterns surface in two weeks. Fix the room, not yourself.
