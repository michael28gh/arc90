### SLIDE 2
[01/05]
mistake #1: you never track anything

⚠ the problem:
You "feel like" you've been consistent. Feelings are the least reliable dataset you own — your brain edits the record every night.

🎯 why it matters:
No log means no feedback loop. You can't debug a system you never measured, so you repeat the same failure blind.

✓ the fix:
Track one binary metric daily: did it happen, yes or no. That's it. 10 seconds a day turns your habit into data you can actually read.

---

### SLIDE 3
[02/05]
mistake #2: running 10 experiments at once

⚠ the problem:
New year energy hits and you change sleep, gym, diet, reading, and meditation simultaneously. Every variable moves at once.

🎯 why it matters:
When it collapses — and it will — you learn nothing. You can't isolate what worked, so the next attempt starts from zero.

✓ the fix:
One variable per protocol cycle. Lock a single habit for 90 days, hold everything else constant. Boring is what compounding looks like from the inside.

---

### SLIDE 4
[03/05]
mistake #3: waiting for motivation

⚠ the problem:
Motivation is a hormone spike, not a strategy. You're outsourcing your protocol to your dopamine levels on any given Tuesday.

🎯 why it matters:
Dopamine baselines fluctuate daily. Build on a fluctuating input and the whole system inherits the noise.

✓ the fix:
Attach the habit to a fixed trigger — same time, same place, same cue. Systems run when motivation doesn't show up. That's the point of a system.

---

### SLIDE 5
[04/05]
mistake #4: phone first, protocol second

⚠ the problem:
First 20 minutes awake, you flood your reward circuitry with cheap dopamine from a feed. Then you ask your brain to enjoy hard things.

🎯 why it matters:
You've front-loaded the day's easiest reward. Everything after competes with it and loses. Your habit never stood a chance by 7 AM.

✓ the fix:
Phone stays untouched until the habit is done. Make the habit the first dopamine of the day. Sequence is a lever — pull it.

---

### SLIDE 6
[05/05]
mistake #5: zero accountability

⚠ the problem:
Private goals have private failures. When nobody sees the data, quitting costs you nothing tonight.

🎯 why it matters:
Self-report to an audience of one is the weakest accountability structure that exists. You'll negotiate with yourself and you'll win.

✓ the fix:
Make the streak visible — a tracker, a friend, a public log. Skin in the game changes the math on skipping.
