### SLIDE 2

[01/05]
mistake #1: running the experiment in a rigged lab
⚠ the problem: Your phone sits on your desk. Your room is optimized for the exact behavior you're trying to quit.
🎯 why it matters: Every visible cue fires a dopamine loop before you make a "decision." Willpower loses to environment every time you're tired — which is daily.
✓ the fix: Audit one room like a scientist. Remove the top 3 cues for your worst habit tonight. Log how often you reach for what's missing.

### SLIDE 3

[02/05]
mistake #2: zero friction on bad inputs
⚠ the problem: Junk food at eye level. Social apps on your home screen. One tap, zero cost.
🎯 why it matters: Behavior follows the path of least resistance, not the path of best intentions. Friction is the variable — not motivation.
✓ the fix: Add 20 seconds of friction to the bad input. Log out, bury the app, move the snacks. Then measure usage for 7 days.

### SLIDE 4

[03/05]
mistake #3: invisible triggers for good habits
⚠ the problem: The habit you want has no cue in the room. You expect memory to fire at 6am. It won't.
🎯 why it matters: No trigger, no behavior — the protocol never starts. You're not lazy; your experiment has no start signal.
✓ the fix: Stage the first rep the night before. Book open on the desk, shoes by the door, bottle filled. Make tomorrow start itself.

### SLIDE 5

[04/05]
mistake #4: new goal, same old environment
⚠ the problem: You set a new protocol but changed nothing physical. Same room, same layout, same inputs.
🎯 why it matters: Your environment already produced your current results. Identical inputs, identical outputs — that's not a mindset issue, that's the system working.
✓ the fix: Change one physical variable per habit. Desk moved, gym bag packed, kitchen restocked. One change, tracked for 90 days.

### SLIDE 6

[05/05]
mistake #5: not tracking the variable
⚠ the problem: You rearranged the room once and called it done. No log, no data, no idea what worked.
🎯 why it matters: Untracked changes can't be repeated or improved. Track it or it didn't happen.
✓ the fix: Log the environment change and the daily outcome next to it. After 90 days you'll know which variable actually moved the number.
