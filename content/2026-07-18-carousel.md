## SLIDE 2 — 01 / 05

**mistake #1: treating sleep as leftover time**
the problem: You schedule workouts, deep work, even meals. Sleep gets whatever hours are left when the day is done.
why it matters: Sleep is the input every other habit runs on. Underslept, your willpower, focus, and mood all trend down together.
the fix: Give sleep a fixed time block like any other protocol. Set a target bedtime, treat it as non-negotiable, and track hits vs. misses.

## SLIDE 3 — 02 / 05

**mistake #2: a random sleep schedule**
the problem: Bed at 10pm Monday, 1am Friday, noon recovery on Sunday. Your body never knows what day it is.
why it matters: An irregular schedule creates social jet lag — the same biology as changing time zones weekly. It flattens energy and makes every habit harder to start.
the fix: Fix your wake time first — same hour, all 7 days. Let bedtime follow naturally. Consistency beats duration.

## SLIDE 4 — 03 / 05

**mistake #3: dopamine right up to lights-out**
the problem: You scroll until your eyes give up. The last input of your day is a feed engineered to keep you awake.
why it matters: Late-night stimulation delays sleep onset and cuts into deep sleep. You lose the exact phase that restores discipline for tomorrow.
the fix: Set a screen cutoff 45–60 minutes before bed. Replace the scroll with one boring analog ritual. Boring is the point.

## SLIDE 5 — 04 / 05

**mistake #4: caffeine after 2pm**
the problem: That 4pm coffee feels harmless. Six hours later, half of it is still in your bloodstream.
why it matters: Caffeine's half-life is roughly 5–6 hours. It rarely stops you falling asleep — it quietly degrades the sleep quality you never notice.
the fix: Run the experiment: caffeine cutoff at 2pm for 14 days. Track sleep quality and morning energy. Compare the data.

## SLIDE 6 — 05 / 05

**mistake #5: not tracking sleep at all**
the problem: You track workouts, macros, screen time — but not the habit that controls all of them. Sleep stays a black box.
why it matters: Untracked, sleep is a guess — and you will always guess high. Track it or it didn't happen.
the fix: Log two numbers daily: bedtime and wake time. That's it. 90 days of data beats any productivity hack.
