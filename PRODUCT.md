# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People running a personal 90-day transformation — fitness, focus, discipline, founders, self-improvement. They open Arc90 on their phone (mobile-first PWA), usually for a few seconds in the morning or at night, to log the day's reps and check whether their momentum is holding. The job: keep habits alive long enough to become identity, and recover fast when a day slips — without willpower being the only thing holding it together.

## Product Purpose

Arc90 turns one meaningful goal into a daily system the user can actually read: track the reps that matter, surface momentum/recovery signals, and make showing up frictionless. Success = the user comes back daily, keeps a streak, and converts to Founding Premium ($49/yr). It is a habit/focus/wellness **tracking** tool — never medical advice.

## Brand Personality

Premium, disciplined, quietly motivating with a touch of tough-love. Think "obsidian luxury fintech for your habits," not a cheerful gamified checklist. Three words: premium, disciplined, momentum. The interface should feel like a pro instrument you trust, not a toy.

## Anti-references

- Generic habit-tracker checklists (cluttered, cheap, list-of-checkboxes).
- Childish/loud gamification (confetti-everything, cartoon mascots, badges-as-noise).
- Clinical/medical UI — Arc90 tracks only and must never look like a health-claims app.
- Default AI-SaaS slop: rainbow gradients everywhere, gradient text as decoration, identical icon-heading-text card grids, tiny tracked uppercase eyebrows on every section, ghost-cards (1px border + big soft shadow), over-rounded 32px+ cards.

## Design Principles

1. **Momentum you can read.** Every screen answers "am I on track?" at a glance — the system does the work, not willpower.
2. **Calm over clutter.** One clear next rep. Protect whitespace; never overwhelm a 5-second morning check-in.
3. **Premium obsidian craft.** Deliberate type scale, restrained color, intentional motion — it should feel expensive and trustworthy.
4. **Reward showing up, never shame.** Streaks, feelings, encouragement — loss-aversion that motivates, not guilt that punishes.
5. **Honest & private by default.** Local-first, no dark patterns, compliance-safe copy — the design reinforces trust.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Dark (primary) and light themes, both must pass contrast (body ≥4.5:1). Mobile-first, large touch targets. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` (the app has streak/float/ring animations — provide reduced alternatives). Never signal state by color alone (pair with label/icon). Legible at a glance for quick daily use.
